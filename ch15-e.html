2
The Story
Read carefully. Notice the bold words.
🐌

The city of Tyre had a secret — and a terrible smell. Visitors who came by sea could smell it before they could even see the city. It was the smell of rotting sea snails. Thousands of them. Tens of thousands. The stench was so powerful that people called Tyre "the smelly city." Yet this same city was also the richest trading city in the ancient world. The secret behind that wealth? Purple.

Along the Phoenician coast lived a small, spiky sea snail called the murex. Inside each snail's body was a tiny gland that produced a small amount of yellow liquid. When this liquid was exposed to sunlight and air, something extraordinary happened: it slowly turned purple — a deep, rich, glorious purple unlike anything nature produced in cloth or stone.

To make purple dye, workers had to crack open murex shells and extract the gland. Then they boiled the glands in large vats. The liquid stank like rotting fish. But the colour it produced was extraordinary. To make enough dye to colour a single garment, workers needed to crush more than 250,000 murex snails.

The result was Tyrian purple — perhaps the most coveted colour in all of history. It did not fade in sunlight. It did not wash out in water. The cloth dyed with it glowed with a depth and richness that no other dye could match.

Because it was so difficult and expensive to produce, purple cloth became the ultimate luxury. It became a symbol of power and status. In Rome, wearing the colour purple without permission from the Emperor was punishable by death. Purple was the imperial colour — the colour of gods and rulers. And it came from a smelly little snail on the coast of Phoenicia.

Language Focus
Grammar from the story — learn through meaning
📌 Superlatives — 'the most... / the -est...'
Use superlatives to describe the extreme of a quality — the highest, best, worst, etc.
Tyrian purple was the most coveted colour in the ancient world.
The stench of Tyre was the worst smell any visitor had ever experienced.
Purple was the richest and most expensive dye known to history.
Tyre was one of the wealthiest cities in the ancient Mediterranean.
📌 Expressing rarity with 'only' and 'just'
Use 'only' and 'just' to emphasise how few or how little.
Only kings and emperors could afford purple cloth.
Each snail produced just one tiny drop of liquid.
Only 250,000 snails could produce enough dye for one garment.
The gland was just a tiny organ inside each shell.
📌 Despite / Although — expressing contrast
Use these words when something unexpected is true.
Despite the terrible stench, Tyre was the richest city in the ancient world.
Although the smell was horrible, workers continued extracting the dye.
Despite the enormous cost, kings and emperors paid whatever it took to own purple cloth.
Although it came from a tiny snail, purple cloth carried enormous power.

Ideas & Society
Think about big ideas. There is no wrong answer.
👗
Clothing as power. Purple was not just a colour — it was a political statement. How do people today use fashion to show their identity, status, or power?
🐌
Extraordinary value from the humblest source. The most coveted colour in human history came from a tiny sea snail. Can you think of other examples where something ordinary becomes extraordinarily valuable?
🏙️
The cost of luxury. Tyre grew wealthy from purple dye, but the workers who extracted the dye endured terrible conditions. Who really paid the price for this luxury?
🌊
Environmental impact of ancient industries. To make enough dye for the Roman Empire, millions of murex snails were harvested. Do you think the ancients thought about environmental sustainability?
