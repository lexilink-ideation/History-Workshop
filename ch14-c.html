The Story
Moses grows up — and the greatest escape in history begins
👨

Moses grew up in the palace of the pharaoh. He ate fine food. He wore fine clothes. But one day, he discovered something that changed his life forever: he was not Egyptian. He was an Israelite.

Moses went out and saw his own people — beaten, working in the hot sun, mistreated. He was angry. So he went to the pharaoh.

"I am an Israelite," he said, "and I worship the one God of the Israelites. God says: Let the Israelites go!"

But the pharaoh refused. He did not want to lose his slaves. So Moses warned him: if the pharaoh would not free the Israelites, God would send ten plagues on Egypt.

🌊

Each plague was a challenge to the Egyptian gods. The Egyptians believed their gods were the most powerful in the world. But one by one, the plagues proved them wrong.

🩸
Plague 1River turned to blood — killing all fish
🐸
Plague 2Frogs everywhere — in beds, food, and clothes
🦟
Plague 3Lice and insects covered the land
🪰
Plague 4Flies swarmed across Egypt
🐂
Plague 5Animals became sick and died
🔴
Plague 6Painful sores appeared on people
⛈️
Plague 7Hail and fire destroyed the crops
🦗
Plague 8Locusts ate all remaining food
🌑
Plague 9Darkness covered Egypt for three days
😢
Plague 10The firstborn son in every Egyptian family died

After the tenth plague, the pharaoh finally said yes. "Go!" he cried. "Take your people and leave!"

That very night, the Israelites packed everything and ran. But then the pharaoh changed his mind. He sent his army after them in their chariots.

🌊

The Israelites ran as fast as they could. But when they looked ahead, they saw water — the Red Sea. And when they looked behind, they saw the dust of the Egyptian army. They were trapped.

"Moses, have you led us out of Egypt only to let us die here by the sea?"

Then Moses raised his staff. God parted the waters. Huge walls of water rose up on each side. The Israelites could see fish swimming inside the walls of water. But the ground beneath their feet was completely dry.

They walked all the way through the Red Sea. Then Moses raised his staff again. The water flooded back — and the entire Egyptian army was drowned.

The Israelites were finally free. This great escape is called the Exodus. It is one of the most important stories in all of history.

The Israelites walked back to Canaan, the land Abraham had once called home. They became a powerful kingdom. And Egypt, once the greatest empire in the world, began to grow weak.

Language Focus
Grammar through the story
📌 Conditional — "if" clauses
We use if to talk about conditions and results.
If the pharaoh refused, God would send plagues.
"Have you led us out of Egypt only to let us die if the army catches us?"
If the pharaoh freed them, they would leave immediately.
📌 Passive voice — "was/were + past participle"
We use passive voice when we focus on what happened TO someone, not who did it.
Active: God drowned the Egyptian army.
Passive: The Egyptian army was drowned.
Active: The pharaoh mistreated the Israelites.
Passive: The Israelites were mistreated by the pharaoh.
📌 Contrast — "but then" / "however" / "finally"
These words show a change or a surprising development.
The pharaoh agreed to let them go. But then he changed his mind.
The plagues came one by one. Finally, the pharaoh agreed.
They were trapped. However, Moses raised his staff and the sea parted.

Ideas & Society
Big questions — think carefully
🔓
The Exodus is one of history's great stories of freedom. Why do human beings value freedom so much? What does it mean to be truly free?
💪
The Israelites were slaves with no weapons. Yet they escaped from the most powerful army in the world. What does this tell us about the limits of power?
🙏
The Exodus shows monotheism winning over polytheism. Why do you think belief in one all-powerful god became more common over time? Does this connect to how humans think about the universe?
📉
Egypt had been the most powerful empire in the world. After the Exodus, it began to grow weak. What makes powerful empires fall? Can you think of any modern examples?

Review + Chapter Project
Chapter 14 — Complete Summary

📖 Chapter 14 in Three Key Points

The Israelites were descendants of Abraham. They moved to Egypt during a famine and were later made into slaves by the Egyptians who feared their growing numbers.
Moses, born an Israelite but raised in the pharaoh's palace, returned to his people and demanded freedom. God sent ten plagues on Egypt to force the pharaoh to agree.
The Israelites escaped through the parted Red Sea in an event called the Exodus. This showed monotheism (one god) winning over polytheism (many gods), and Egypt beginning to weaken as a power.

⭐ Star Words — Chapter 14

⭐ monotheist⭐ famine⭐ slave⭐ plague⭐ Exodus⭐ courage⭐ sacred⭐ conquer
💡 The Big Idea

The Exodus story teaches us that power is not permanent. Egypt was the strongest empire in the world — yet a group of slaves with no weapons escaped it. History shows us again and again that empires rise, and empires fall. This idea will appear in many of your future IELTS questions.
