2
The Story
Read carefully. Notice the bold words.
⚔️

Long ago, a great king called Shamshi-Adad had built the first Assyrian Empire. He wanted to rule the whole world. His armies conquered cities all around him. But when the Assyrians fought the Babylonians, they lost. They were forced to obey the king of Babylon. Yet all the time, a fire burned in their hearts: "One day, we will be free again."

That day finally came. The Assyrians rebelled against their masters. They dug canals through the city of Babylon and flooded it — washing the great city away with water. Then they began to rebuild their power.

We are like an evil rain that washes its enemies away! We are like a net that tangles the feet of those who fight against us!

The Assyrian armies raged up and down the Tigris and Euphrates rivers, taking every city in their path. They marched into Canaan and scattered the Israelites — those people were never allowed to return home. They forced people in Asia Minor to obey them. And then came their greatest triumph: the king called Ashurbanipal led his soldiers all the way down into Egypt and took it over. Even the mighty pharaohs had to bow to Assyria.

Ashurbanipal became king around 668 BC. He terrified his enemies. For fun, he went on lion-hunts, chasing lions on horseback and shooting arrows at them. In battle, he fought like an angry lion himself. His soldiers fought in clever pairs — one held a light basket shield, the other shot arrows from behind it. They marched side by side like a moving wall with arrows flying out.

But Ashurbanipal was not just powerful — he was ruthless. He burned houses, smashed walls, and scattered salt in the fields of people he conquered, poisoning the land so that no crops would grow. He took people as slaves and never let them go home. Most cities were too frightened to resist. They obeyed him — not because they admired him, but because they feared him.

Language Focus
Grammar from the story — learn through meaning
📌 Irregular Past Tense — Strong Verbs of Power
History stories use many irregular past tense verbs. You must memorise these forms.
BasePastFrom the story
becomebecameAshurbanipal became king around 668 BC.
leadledHe led his soldiers all the way to Egypt.
fightfoughtHe fought like an angry lion.
taketookHe took people as slaves.
buildbuiltShamshi-Adad built the Assyrian Empire.
📌 "so that" — Expressing Purpose
Use "so that" to explain WHY someone did something — the purpose or goal.
He scattered salt in their fields so that no crops would grow.
The soldiers held shields so that they could shoot arrows safely.
Cities obeyed Ashurbanipal so that he would not destroy them.
The Assyrians dug canals so that the water would flood Babylon.
📌 Contrast with "but" and "however"
Use these words to show an unexpected or opposite result.
Ashurbanipal was very rich and powerful, but all across Assyria, people hated him.
The Assyrians lost to Babylon. However, they never gave up their dream of freedom.
Cities obeyed him. But all the time, they hoped Assyria would collapse.

8
Ideas & Society
Think about big ideas. There is no wrong answer.
⚔️
Power and fear. Ashurbanipal's subjects obeyed him out of fear, not love. Do you think a leader who is feared is more powerful than one who is loved? What are the risks of ruling through fear?
🌊
Destruction as a weapon. The Assyrians flooded cities, salted fields, and took people as slaves. Why do you think ancient armies used these tactics? Are any of these tactics still used in conflicts today?
🌍
Empires rise and fall. The Assyrians were conquered by Babylon, rose again, and eventually fell again. Why do you think empires never last forever? What causes powerful empires to collapse?
😤
Hatred and resistance. One conquered person wrote: "When we finally hear the news of your destruction, we will clap our hands with joy!" What does this tell us about how conquest affects the people who are conquered?

💡 Big Idea

Ashurbanipal built the largest empire the world had ever seen — but he built it on fear. Power that comes only from terror is fragile: all across his lands, people were waiting and hoping for the day Assyria would fall.
