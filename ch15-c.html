The Story
Read carefully. Notice the bold words.
🔥

The story of glass begins with a campfire on a beach. According to legend, a group of Phoenician merchants were cooking their meal on the sand beside the sea. They propped their cooking pots on blocks of lye — a hard white mineral made from wood ash. The fire grew very hot. The lye began to melt. It mixed with the sand beneath. And when the fire cooled, the merchants saw something they had never seen before: a small pool of shimmering, clear liquid that had hardened into something strangely beautiful. They had made glass.

Whether the legend is true or not, we know this: the Phoenicians were among the world's first glassmakers. They experimented. They built special furnaces — chambers of intense heat — to melt sand and lye together deliberately. When the mixture became molten, a glowing orange liquid, they could shape it.

The earliest Phoenician glass objects were beads — tiny, colourful, and precious. Craftsmen shaped molten glass around a clay core, adding threads of colour. When the glass cooled and hardened, they dug out the clay. What remained was a beautiful little vessel.

The Phoenicians also poured molten glass into moulds to create bowls, bottles, and perfume flasks. Each object was unique. Each one shimmered and caught the light. Because glass was transparent, you could see straight through it — which seemed almost magical in the ancient world.

Glass was extraordinarily precious. A single glass bowl could be worth more than gold. Only kings and pharaohs could afford it. The craftsmen who knew the secret of glass guarded it carefully. And the Phoenicians who could make it and trade it grew rich beyond measure.

Language Focus
Grammar from the story — learn through meaning
📌 Passive Voice — 'was / were + past participle'
Use the passive voice when the action matters more than who did it.
Glass was discovered by accident on a Phoenician beach.
Moulds were used to shape the molten glass.
The clay core was removed after the glass cooled.
The secret of glassmaking was guarded carefully.
📌 Sequence connectives — First, Then, After that, Finally
Use these words to describe a process in order.
First, craftsmen heated sand and lye in a furnace.
Then, the mixture became molten — a glowing liquid.
After that, they poured it into moulds or shaped it around a clay core.
Finally, when the glass cooled, they removed the mould or clay.
📌 Expressing value — 'worth' and 'more... than'
Use these structures to compare the value of things.
A glass bowl was worth more than gold.
Glassmaking knowledge was more valuable than an army.
Coloured glass beads were worth a fortune in Egypt.
The secret of making glass was priceless in the ancient world.

Ideas & Society
Think about big ideas. There is no wrong answer.
🔥
Discovery often happens by accident. Glass was supposedly discovered when a fire accidentally melted sand. Can you think of other important discoveries that happened by accident?
💎
Value is created by rarity and beauty. Glass was ordinary sand — but transformed by skill and fire into something precious. How do humans decide what is valuable?
🤫
The Phoenicians guarded the secret of glassmaking. Controlling a technology gives you enormous power. Do you think companies or countries today do the same with important technologies?
🏺
Objects can carry meaning beyond their physical form. A glass bowl was not just for drinking from — it was a symbol of wealth and power. What objects today carry similar symbolic meaning?
