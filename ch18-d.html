The Story
Read carefully. Notice the bold words.
👑

The Minoans, who lived on the island of Crete, were named after a legendary king called Minos. And the Minoans told an unforgettable story about him.

Minos, they said, was a son of Zeus, the chief of all the gods — but because he was only half-human, he could not live among the gods themselves. Instead, he lived on Crete, in a great and beautiful palace.

🌀 But that shining palace hid a dark secret. Deep below its foundations lay a labyrinth so twisted and complicated that no one who entered could ever find their way out again. And somewhere in the darkest heart of that labyrinth lived a horrible monster — the Minotaur, half man and half bull, who was said to eat human beings.

😨 King Minos refused to feed his own people to the monster. Instead, he threatened the nearby city of Athens: every year, Athens must send him seven girls and seven boys as tribute, or he would destroy the entire city. Year after year, the terrified Athenians placed the names of all their children into a bowl, drew out fourteen unlucky victims, and sent them by ship to Crete — never to be seen again.

⚓ On his eighteenth birthday, a young man named Theseus, son of the king of Athens, walked down to the seaside. The sky was clear and the sun was shining — but the beach was crowded with weeping mothers and fathers, standing beside a ship with black sails.

"Why does the ship have black sails? Why is everyone crying?" Theseus asked.

"Because our sons and daughters are being sent to Crete," one mother answered. "They will be eaten by the Minotaur, and we will never see them again."

Theseus was horrified. "Why did I never know about this?" he demanded.

"Because you are the prince," someone told him. "Your name is never placed in the bowl with the others."

😤 "But that isn't right!" Theseus said. "Let me go to Crete in place of one of the young men. I will face the Minotaur myself, and try to kill him. If I succeed, we will fly a white sail instead of black, and no one will ever be sacrificed to King Minos again."

King Aegeus begged his son not to go — but Theseus was determined, and in the end, he had his way. He boarded the ship with the other victims and set sail for Crete, carrying with him the fear of a nation and the hope that he might finally end its terrible tribute forever.

Language Focus
Grammar from the story — learn through meaning
📌 Reported speech — retelling dialogue from history and myth
When we report what someone said, we often change the tense and pronouns.
Theseus asked why the ship had black sails.
The mother said that her children were being sent to Crete.
Theseus said that he would face the Minotaur himself.
📌 First conditional — talking about future possibilities
Use 'if' + present simple, + 'will' to describe a likely future result.
If I succeed, we will fly a white sail instead of black.
If Theseus kills the Minotaur, the tribute will end forever.
If Athens refuses, King Minos will destroy the city.
📌 Modal verb 'must' — expressing a strong obligation
Use 'must' to show that something is required or forced upon someone.
Every year, Athens must send fourteen young people to Crete.
Theseus felt that he must do something to stop the tribute.
No one must speak the prince's name in the bowl of victims.

Ideas & Society
Think about big ideas. There is no wrong answer.
⚖️
King Minos ruled through fear and threats. What are the dangers of a ruler who controls people through fear rather than respect?
👦
Theseus chose to risk his life to save strangers. What do you think makes some people willing to sacrifice themselves for others?
🎭
This story blends real history (the Minoans) with myth (the Minotaur). Why do you think ancient cultures often mixed real events with legendary monsters?
🗳️
Athenian families drew names from a bowl to choose victims. Do you think this method was fair, and can any method make an unfair situation feel fair?
