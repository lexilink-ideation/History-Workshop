The Story
Read carefully. Notice the bold words.
👑

Long ago, in the great Phoenician city of Tyre, there lived a princess named Dido. Dido was beautiful, wise, and as clever as any king. But her world was about to fall apart.

When Dido's father died, her brother Pygmalion became king. Pygmalion was cruel and greedy. He murdered Dido's husband to steal his gold. Dido knew she was in terrible danger. So she gathered her most loyal friends, loaded their ships with her husband's treasure, and did the only thing she could: she fled.

Dido's ships sailed west across the Mediterranean. They passed islands and coastlines until they reached the northern shore of Africa. There, Dido asked the local king for a small piece of land — just enough to give her people a home. The king smiled. "You may have," he said, "as much land as an ox hide can cover."

He thought he was being clever. How much land could one ox hide cover? But Dido was more cunning than he knew. She took the ox hide and cut it into hundreds of incredibly thin strips. Then she tied the strips end to end into one long line and laid them in a great circle on the ground. Inside that circle of leather strips, she founded her city.

The king could not believe it. The bargain had been kept — technically. And inside that circle of ox hide, Dido built the city of Carthage. She became its founder and its first queen. She reigned wisely and well. The city grew powerful and rich.

Carthage would go on to become Rome's greatest rival — a maritime empire that stretched across the western Mediterranean. All because one woman, using only her wit and a single ox hide, claimed a kingdom from nothing.

📌 Despite / In spite of — expressing contrast
Use these phrases when an outcome is surprising given the circumstances.
Despite having no army, Dido claimed a kingdom using only her wit.
In spite of losing everything in Tyre, Dido built a great city in Africa.
Despite the king thinking the bargain was in his favour, Dido outsmarted him.
In spite of being a refugee, Dido became a queen.
📌 What if...? — Conditional sentences
Use 'If... would...' to imagine different possibilities in history.
If Dido had not fled Tyre, Carthage might never have been founded.
If the king had thought more carefully, he would not have made the bargain.
If Carthage had not existed, Rome might have dominated the Mediterranean even sooner.
If Dido had used an army instead of her wit, she might have failed.

Ideas & Society
Think about big ideas. There is no wrong answer.
🧠
Wit is more powerful than weapons. Dido had no army, yet she founded a kingdom. Can you think of other historical figures who used intelligence rather than force to change their situation?
🏃
Dido was a refugee who became a queen. She lost her home, fled danger, and built something new. How does her story make you feel about refugees and displaced people today?
⚖️
She kept the letter of the bargain, but not the spirit. Dido technically did what was agreed, but the king felt tricked. When is a clever trick also a kind of dishonesty?
🌆
Great cities are often founded by outsiders. Carthage was founded by a refugee princess. Can you think of other cities or nations founded by people who came from somewhere else?

📖 Chapter 15 Summary

The Phoenicians lived on a narrow coastal strip between the mountains and the Mediterranean Sea. With almost no farmland, they turned to the sea and became the world's greatest traders. They built great harbour cities — Tyre, Sidon, and Byblos — and sailed trade routes from Egypt to Britain. They invented glassmaking and glassblowing, discovered the precious purple dye from the murex snail, and founded colonies across the Mediterranean, most famously Carthage. Above all, they gave the world the alphabet — the foundation of almost every writing system used today. Their greatest queen, Dido, used nothing but her wit to found a new kingdom from nothing. The Phoenicians built no great pyramids and left no stone temples — yet their legacy touches every word you write.

💡 The Big Idea of Chapter 15

The Phoenicians had almost nothing. No river. No vast farmland. No empire. Yet they gave the world glass, the purple dye of kings, and — most importantly — the alphabet. Creativity, skill, and trade can build a legacy more lasting than any army or empire.
