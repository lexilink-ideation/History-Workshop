The Story
Read carefully. Notice the bold words.
⚓

The Minoans were famous for more than just bull-jumping — they were also the greatest shipbuilders of the ancient world. And that skill turned out to be just as important to their survival as anything happening in the bull-jumping arena.

🏴‍☠️ In ancient times, the Mediterranean Sea was a dangerous place. No king or country truly ruled the waves; instead, the sea was controlled by pirates and bandits who sailed small boats close to the shore. These pirates attacked and robbed almost anyone who dared to venture out onto the water. Kings of the ancient world had learned how to build powerful armies that could fight on land — but none of them knew how to build ships big and strong enough to carry soldiers safely out to sea. So the pirates ruled the Mediterranean, and trade across the water remained extremely risky.

👑 But the king of the Minoans saw things differently. He understood that the Minoans needed to sail safely across the Mediterranean if they ever hoped to trade with other lands and grow wealthy. So he ordered his craftsmen to build great ships — ships strong enough to hunt down pirates and patrol the open sea.

🛠️ The Minoan craftsmen rose to the challenge. They became the finest shipbuilders anywhere in the ancient world, and their king became the very first ruler to command a true navy — an army built specifically to fight on water. This powerful navy swept the pirates from the Mediterranean and wiped them out, clearing safe pathways for Minoan traders to sail to distant lands, buy and sell goods, and bring wealth home to Crete.

🏛️ The Minoan navy grew so strong that it changed the way the Minoans lived on land. The greatest palace on Crete was never even surrounded by protective walls — a bold choice in the ancient world, where most rulers feared invasion at any moment. But no invaders could reach Crete's shores. The navy stood guard on the water, and it never let them through, keeping the whole island protected for generations.

Language Focus
Grammar from the story — learn through meaning
📌 Superlatives — describing 'the greatest,' 'the first'
Use superlatives to describe something as the most extreme example of its kind.
The Minoan craftsmen were the greatest shipbuilders of the ancient world.
The king became the first ruler to have a navy.
The Minoan navy became the strongest in the world.
📌 'So that' — showing purpose
Use 'so that' to explain the reason or purpose behind an action.
He ordered his craftsmen to build ships so that the Minoans could sail safely.
The navy patrolled the sea so that pirates could never return.
Strong ships were built so that traders could carry goods to distant countries.
📌 Cause and effect with 'because' — explaining historical results
Use 'because' to explain why something happened.
The palace had no walls because the navy protected the shore.
Trade grew because the sea was finally safe.
The Minoans became wealthy because their ships could travel freely.
