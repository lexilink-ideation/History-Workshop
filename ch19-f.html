The Story
Read carefully. Notice the bold words.
🏹

For hundreds of years, the Mycenaean Greeks ruled the Aegean Sea, confident that their bronze weapons and chariots made them unbeatable. But the Mycenaean Greeks were facing disaster — although they didn't know it yet!

All around them, tribes of barbarians were also learning how to use bronze weapons and chariots. And then the barbarians discovered something even stronger: iron. These iron weapons were tougher than anything bronze could match.

They learned how to use bows and arrows, and how to throw javelins. Now, the barbarians could kill the drivers of Greek chariots from a long distance away — before the Greeks could even get close enough to fight back.

Some barbarians even learned how to build warships, so that they could attack the Mycenaean Greeks from the sea itself. These sea-faring barbarians were called the Sea People. (Some of them later settled in Canaan, and became known as the Philistines.)

The Greeks tried to fight off the barbarians. They built stronger and stronger walls around their cities — walls so strong that we can still see their ruins today. But no matter how many walls they built, the Mycenaean Greeks could not stop what was coming.

Language Focus
Grammar from the story — learn through meaning
📌 Past Simple for sequence of events
Use simple past tense to narrate a clear sequence of historical events, one after another.
The barbarians discovered iron.
They learned how to use bows and arrows.
The Greeks built stronger walls.
💡 Each simple past verb moves the story forward to the next event.
📌 'Before' and 'after' to sequence events
'Before' and 'after' help readers understand the exact order events happened in.
Barbarians could kill chariot drivers before the Greeks even got close.
After the Sea People attacked, the Greeks built stronger walls.
Before this, bronze weapons had always been the strongest.
💡 These time words connect two related events in a clear timeline.
📌 Modal 'could' for past ability
Use 'could' to talk about someone's ability, or inability, to do something in the past.
Barbarians could kill drivers from a long distance.
Some barbarians could build warships.
The Greeks could not keep the barbarians out.
💡 'Could not' shows a past limitation, even after great effort.

Ideas & Society
Think about big ideas. There is no wrong answer.
🏹
A new invention — iron — completely changed the balance of power in the Aegean. Can you think of a modern example where a single new technology has quickly shifted power between nations?
🚢
The Sea People used warships to attack Mycenaean Greeks from a direction they didn't expect. Why do you think attacking from an unexpected direction is such a powerful military strategy?
🧱
The Greeks responded to danger by building bigger and stronger walls. Do you think building bigger defenses is usually enough to stop a determined enemy? Why or why not?
🌍
Some Sea People settled permanently in Canaan and became known as the Philistines. What do you think happens to a group's identity when they conquer and settle in a completely new land?
