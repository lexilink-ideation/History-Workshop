The Story
Read carefully. Notice the bold words.
🌿

Long, long ago, a man named Abraham lived in a place called Ur. Abraham heard a voice — he believed it was the voice of one god, the only true god. Abraham became a monotheist: a person who believes in only one god. This was very unusual. Most people in the ancient world were polytheists. They believed in many gods.

Abraham had a son named Isaac. Isaac had a son named Jacob. Jacob had twelve sons. Each son had a big family. Over time, all these families grew into a whole new nation — the nation of Israel. The people were called Israelites.

God told the Israelites to live in a land called Canaan. It was a dry and rocky place, but they tried to stay there. Then one day, a terrible famine came. It stopped raining. Plants would not grow. Animals died. The Israelites had no food.

Do you remember Joseph? His brothers sold him as a slave in Egypt. But Joseph became very important there. He had lots of grain and food. So he invited all the Israelites to come to Egypt.

The Israelites packed all their belongings — their tents, their animals, their families — and walked down to Egypt. They started to live on the banks of the Nile River and worshipped their one god.

At first, the Egyptians were happy to have them. But the Israelite nation kept growing and growing. Soon the Egyptians became afraid. "What if these people attack us?" they said. "What if they take our kingdom?"

So the Egyptians did something very cruel: they made the Israelites into slaves. The Israelites had to make heavy mud bricks. They were not paid. They were not free. They were forced to obey the Egyptians in everything.

🧱

But even as slaves, the Israelite nation kept growing. The Egyptians saw them as a threat. The stage was set for one of the most famous stories in all of history...

Language Focus
Grammar from the story — learn through meaning
📌 Past Tense — Regular & Irregular
We use the past tense to talk about things that already happened.
Base formPast tenseFrom the story
believebelievedAbraham believed in one god.
growgrewThe nation grew larger and larger.
packpackedThey packed all their belongings.
makemadeThe Egyptians made them into slaves.
havehadJacob had twelve sons.
📌 Reason Clauses — "because" and "so"
Use because to explain WHY. Use so to show what happened as a result.
A famine came, so the Israelites had no food.
The Egyptians were afraid, so they made the Israelites into slaves.
The Israelites went to Egypt because Joseph had food there.
They had to obey the Egyptians because they were slaves.
📌 Sequence — "at first" / "but then"
These phrases show how a situation CHANGED over time.
At first, the Egyptians were happy to have the Israelites.
But then the Israelites grew larger and larger.
But then the Egyptians became afraid and made them slaves.

Ideas & Society
Think about big ideas. There is no wrong answer.
🏛️
Why do powerful groups sometimes fear weaker groups? Think about history. Have you seen this happen in other places too?
⚖️
Is it ever fair to make someone a slave because you are afraid of them? What should powerful people do when they feel threatened?
🌍
The Israelites believed in only one god. Most people around them believed in many gods. Do you think it was difficult to be different from everyone around you? Why?
🍞
When there is a famine, people move to find food. This still happens today. Do you think countries should help people who are fleeing famine? Why or why not?

💡 Think About It

The Israelites were peaceful people. They simply had many children. But this made the Egyptians afraid. Fear can turn ordinary people into oppressors. Remember this idea — you will see it again in history, and in the IELTS exam.
