The Story
Read carefully. Notice the bold words.
👑

Even though Nebuchadnezzar had rebuilt his empire and ruled a mighty kingdom, he was not able to relax. Sitting alone on his throne one evening, he found himself worrying. His empire was enormous and powerful — but what if another country decided to attack it? He was not entirely confident that his own army could successfully defend Babylon from a determined invader.

One country worried him more than any other: Persia, a rising power to the east. The Persians were rapidly expanding their own territory, and their soldiers had a fearsome reputation. Nebuchadnezzar had heard many frightening stories about Persian invaders, and the thought of facing them in battle made him deeply uneasy.

Then an idea occurred to him. “I know what I will do,” he thought to himself. “I will ask the king of Persia if I may marry his daughter. If I do, he will become my father-in-law — and surely no father-in-law would ever send an army to attack his own son-in-law's kingdom!”

Nebuchadnezzar had never even seen the Persian princess. It did not matter to him. He was perfectly willing to marry a complete stranger if it meant keeping Babylon safe from war. So he sent official messages to the king of Persia, formally offering to marry the princess.

Remarkably, at that very same moment, the king of Persia was sitting on his own throne, worrying about the exact same danger — but in reverse. “What will happen if Babylon attacks us?” he thought anxiously. “Their soldiers are excellent fighters. I am not sure our forces could defeat them. How can I possibly convince Nebuchadnezzar to leave my kingdom alone?”

Just then, a servant hurried in with Nebuchadnezzar's message. “Sir,” the servant announced, “the king of Babylon has sent an offer of marriage. He wishes to know whether you will give him your daughter.”

The Persian king was tremendously relieved. “Of course I will!” he exclaimed. “Nebuchadnezzar would never dare to attack his own father-in-law!” He immediately sent for his daughter, the princess Amytis. “My dear,” he told her gently, “the king of Babylon wishes to marry you.”

Amytis, however, was far less pleased. “But I have never even met him!” she protested. “And I do not want to leave my home in the mountains to live in Babylon. Down there on the flat land, the air is thick and still.”

“If you marry him,” her father explained patiently, “Persia will be safe from attack. You would be protecting your entire country.” After much thought, Amytis finally, reluctantly, agreed.

Language Focus
Grammar from the story — learn through meaning
📌 Reported thought — 'thought to himself'
We can show a character's private thoughts directly, or report them indirectly.
Direct: He thought to himself, “I know what I'll do.”
Reported: He thought that he knew what to do.
Direct: “What will happen if Babylon attacks us?” he thought.
Reported: He wondered what would happen if Babylon attacked.
📌 First conditional — real future possibility
Use 'if + present simple, ... will + verb' to talk about a realistic future result.
If I marry his daughter, he will become my father-in-law.
If you marry him, Persia will be safe.
If Babylon attacks, we will lose many soldiers.
📌 'Would never' — expressing strong hypothetical certainty
Use 'would never' to show complete confidence that something will not happen.
Nebuchadnezzar would never attack his own father-in-law.
A father-in-law would never send an army against his own family.
She would never have agreed if peace hadn't been at stake.

Ideas & Society
Think about big ideas. There is no wrong answer.
💍
Political marriages, like the one between Nebuchadnezzar and Amytis, were common in ancient times. Do you think using marriage to prevent war was a clever strategy or a cruel one?
⚖️
Amytis had very little choice in her own marriage. How do you think decisions about marriage and family have changed between ancient times and today?
🕊️
Both kings were afraid of each other, yet neither one actually attacked. Do you think fear can sometimes prevent war just as effectively as friendship?
👪
Amytis was asked to protect her whole country through her personal sacrifice. Can you think of other historical examples where one person's personal choice affected an entire nation?
