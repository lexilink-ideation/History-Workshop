The Story
Read carefully. Notice the bold words.
🌊

Look at a map of the ancient world. On the eastern shore of the Mediterranean Sea, squeezed between tall mountains and crashing waves, there was a narrow strip of land. The mountains were so close to the sea that there was almost no room to farm. The soil was scarce and rocky. Yet from this tight little strip of land rose one of the most remarkable civilisations in all of history — the Phoenicians.

The Phoenicians lived in great coastal cities: Tyre, Sidon, and Byblos. Each city had its own king. Each city had a great harbour where ships could dock, be loaded with goods, and sail out into the vast sea.

The Phoenicians had a problem: they had almost no farmland. They could not grow enough food for their growing population. But they had something extraordinary — the Lebanon Mountains rising behind them, covered in towering cedar trees. And in front of them — the sea.

So the Phoenicians made a choice. Instead of farming, they would expand outward. They became merchants — buying and selling goods across the ancient world. They became sailors. They mastered the art of navigation, learning to steer their ships by the stars at night.

The Egyptians called the Phoenicians the purple people. The Greeks called them the alphabet people. But everyone agreed on one thing: the Phoenicians were the greatest sailors and traders of the ancient world. In the coming lessons, you will discover why — and how a small, mountainous strip of land changed history forever.

Language Focus
Grammar from the story — learn through meaning
📌 Past Tense — Regular & Irregular
We use the past tense to talk about finished actions in history.
BasePastFrom the story
livelivedThe Phoenicians lived on a narrow strip of land.
sailsailedThey sailed across the Mediterranean.
riseroseA great civilisation rose from that strip of land.
havehadThey had almost no farmland.
becomebecameThey became the greatest sailors in the world.
📌 Contrast — 'but' and 'however'
Use 'but' and 'however' to show two opposite or surprising ideas.
The land was rocky and scarce, but the Phoenicians built a great civilisation.
They had no farmland. However, they had cedar trees and the sea.
The mountains blocked them on one side, but the sea opened up the whole world.
📌 Prepositional phrases of place
Use prepositions to describe exactly WHERE something is.
The Phoenicians lived on the eastern shore of the Mediterranean.
Their cities were between the mountains and the sea.
Cedar trees grew on the slopes of the Lebanon Mountains.
Merchants sailed across the Mediterranean to trade.

Ideas & Society
Think about big ideas. There is no wrong answer.
🏔️
Geography shapes civilisation. The Phoenicians had no farmland, so they took to the sea. Can you think of another civilisation where geography forced people to develop special skills?
🌊
The sea can be both dangerous and full of opportunity. The Phoenicians chose to sail into the unknown. Do you think it takes courage to become a sailor? What risks would you face?
🌲
The Phoenicians had cedar trees — a resource other nations desperately needed. Why do you think nations with scarce resources often become great traders? Can you think of a modern example?
🗺️
Trade connects people across cultures. When the Phoenicians sailed to Egypt or Greece, they brought not only goods but also ideas. Do you think trade is still an important way cultures connect today?

💡 Big Idea

The Phoenicians had almost nothing — no farmland, no great river. But they had the sea, the cedar, and the will to sail. Sometimes the greatest civilisations come from places where survival itself demands creativity.
