Who Were the Phoenicians?

The greatest sailors of the ancient world — and why they took to the sea

2 — The Story
Read carefully. Notice the bold words.
🌊

After the great Exodus, the Israelites walked north from Egypt back to Canaan — the land that Abraham had once called home. But when they arrived, they found that they were not the only people living there. Others had made Canaan their home too.

In the northern part of Canaan lived a people called the Phoenicians. And they were unlike any other people in the ancient world. Why? Because they were the greatest sailors who had ever lived.

To understand why the Phoenicians took to the sea, you have to understand their land. Northern Canaan — the land of the Phoenicians — was a narrow strip of coastal territory. On one side: the blue Mediterranean Sea. On the other: steep, craggy hills that made it almost impossible to travel inland.

🪨

The land itself was a problem. It was rocky and sandy and dry — not at all fertile. You could not grow wheat there. You could not raise fat, healthy animals. And you could not easily escape by land, because the hills were too steep and rough.

So what did the Phoenicians do? They did what clever people do when the land fails them: they adapted. They looked at that long stretch of blue water in front of them, and they thought: "We will go to sea."

They built boats. Then they built better boats. They practised sailing close to the shore, then further out, then all the way across the open Mediterranean. They learned to navigate by the stars at night. They learned to read the winds and the currents.

⚓

Soon, the Phoenicians were the best sailors in the world. Their ships pushed out into the sea and reached places no other people had visited — down the coast of Africa, across to Spain, and perhaps even further.

They built harbours — safe places where ships could stop and rest. And wherever their ships went, they brought goods to trade. They became merchants of the sea: buying and selling, sailing and exploring, connecting the ancient world in ways nobody had done before.

The isolation that their craggy hills had created was now their greatest strength. Because they could not travel easily by land, they had mastered the sea. And the sea belonged to no nation — it was open to anyone brave enough to sail it.

The Phoenicians were brave enough.

4 — Language Focus
Grammar from the story — learn through meaning
📌 GRAMMAR
Because / So — Showing Cause and Effect

We use because to give a reason. We use so to show a result.

The land was not fertile, so the Phoenicians turned to the sea.
Because the land was poor, the Phoenicians adapted and became sailors.
The hills were craggy, so it was hard to travel by land.
Notice: "because" introduces a reason clause. "So" introduces a result clause. Both connect two ideas.
📌 GRAMMAR
Could / Could not — Expressing Ability in the Past

We use could to talk about ability in the past — what was possible or impossible.

SentenceMeaning
The Phoenicians could navigate by the stars.They had this skill.
They could not grow wheat on rocky land.It was impossible.
Ships could stop safely in the harbours.It was possible.
People could not easily travel through the hills.The hills blocked the way.
📌 GRAMMAR
Contrast — "But" and "However"

These words connect two contrasting ideas — they show when something is surprising or unexpected.

The land was poor. But the Phoenicians became wealthy.
The hills isolated them. However, this isolation became their greatest strength.
In writing, use "however" with a comma after it. "But" usually goes in the middle of a sentence.

8 — Ideas & Society
Think about big ideas. There is no wrong answer.
🗺️

Geography shapes history. The Phoenicians became sailors because of where they lived. How do you think the geography of a place affects the way its people live, work, and think? Can you think of other examples from history or from your own life?

🌊

The sea as a highway. Before roads and railways, the sea was the fastest way to travel long distances. Why do you think controlling sea routes gave certain civilizations so much power? Is the sea still important today?

💪

Turning weakness into strength. The Phoenicians had poor land and difficult hills — but these very problems pushed them to master the sea. Do you believe that challenges make people stronger? Why or why not?

🤝

Traders connect the world. By sailing from country to country, the Phoenicians helped different cultures share ideas, art, and technology. Do you think trade between nations brings people together or causes problems? Explain your view.
