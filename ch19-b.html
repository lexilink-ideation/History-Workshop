The Story
Read carefully. Notice the bold words.
⚓

Now the Mycenaeans owned both their own city and the island of Crete. They learned how to build ships from the Minoans who were left in Crete.

Look at a map of Greece. It juts out into the Mediterranean Sea like a set of fingers, surrounded by small islands in every direction. It was the perfect place for a nation of sailors.

The Mycenaeans used their new ships to sail to these islands, one after another. On each island, they built a city called a colony.

All of these cities were controlled by the Mycenaean king and his army. Soon the Mycenaeans had colonies all around the Aegean Sea, and their small kingdom had grown into something far greater.

The Mycenaeans weren't the only people who lived in Greece, though. Other Greeks lived in the cities of Thebes and Athens. But the Mycenaeans had one advantage that set them apart from every other Greek city — and it wasn't ships.

Language Focus
Grammar from the story — learn through meaning
📌 Passive Voice — 'was/were + past participle'
Use the passive voice when the action matters more than who did it.
All of these cities were controlled by the Mycenaean king.
Ships were built using skills learned from the Minoans.
Colonies were established all around the Aegean Sea.
💡 Passive voice puts the focus on the cities and colonies, not on the king himself.
📌 Prepositions of place — describing geography
Prepositions like 'north of,' 'into,' and 'around' help describe exact locations.
Greece is the land just north of Crete.
It juts out into the Mediterranean Sea.
Colonies were built all around the Aegean Sea.
💡 Precise prepositions make geography descriptions much clearer.
📌 Infinitive of purpose — 'to + verb'
Use 'to + verb' to explain WHY someone does something.
They used these ships to sail to other islands.
The Mycenaeans learned shipbuilding to expand their power.
They built colonies to control trade around the Aegean Sea.
💡 This structure links an action directly to its purpose or goal.

Ideas & Society
Think about big ideas. There is no wrong answer.
⚓
Sailing and trade allowed the Mycenaeans to expand far beyond their home city. How important do you think transportation technology, like ships, is for a civilization's growth?
🏰
The Mycenaean king controlled every colony from a distance. What challenges do you think a king would face trying to control colonies far across the sea?
🗺️
The Mycenaeans borrowed shipbuilding skills from the very people they conquered. Why do you think conquerors often learn from and adopt the skills of the people they defeat?
🌊
The Aegean Sea connected many different islands and cities. Do you think seas and oceans usually connect people more than they divide them? Give examples.
