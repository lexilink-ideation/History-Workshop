The Story
A mother hides her baby to keep him safe
👶

The pharaoh of Egypt sat on his throne. He looked angry. He was thinking about the Israelites. "There are more and more of them every day!" he thought. "Soon they will take over Egypt. I must do something."

Then he had a terrible, dreadful idea. He called his soldiers. "Go to every Israelite home," he said. "Find out when the mothers are having babies. Then kill every baby boy!"

When the Israelites heard this, they mourned and cried. "God, save us!" they prayed. "Send someone to deliver us from this wicked pharaoh!"

🌿

An Israelite woman who lived near the Nile River was getting ready to have her baby. When she heard the terrible order, she hid in a back room. The baby was born — a fine, strong baby boy.

For three months, the mother and baby hid from the soldiers. But babies grow. They get louder. Soon, the mother knew she could not hide him forever. She had to be very brave — she needed great courage.

So she found some tall reeds near the river. She wove them into a basket. Then she coated the outside with tar so that it would float on water. She wrapped the baby in a warm, soft blanket and placed him gently inside the basket.

She pushed the basket out onto the Nile River. Then she sent her daughter Miriam to stand by the riverbank and watch what would happen.

The baby drifted slowly down the river. The gentle rocking of the basket lulled him to sleep. The basket floated closer and closer to the edge of the river, until it caught in some weeds.

👑

The daughter of the pharaoh came to the river to swim. She saw the basket. "What is in that basket?" she asked. One of her maids ran to get it.

The princess looked inside. She saw the baby boy. He opened his eyes and smiled at her. "Oh!" she cried. "Such a beautiful baby! I will keep him and raise him as my own!"

Miriam saw what happened. She ran forward bravely. "Lady," she said, "shall I find you a nurse to take care of the baby?"

"Yes!" said the princess. "Bring her to me."

So Miriam ran home and brought back her own mother. The mother and baby were together again! She took care of him until he was old enough to live in the palace. The pharaoh's daughter named him Moses.

Language Focus
Grammar through the story
📌 Sequence words — so that / until / when
These words connect actions and show order or purpose.
She coated the basket with tar so that it would float.
She hid the baby until he was three months old.
When the princess looked inside, she saw the baby.
📌 Reported speech — direct vs indirect
When we tell someone what another person said, we can use direct speech (with quotation marks) or indirect speech (no quotation marks).
Direct: The princess said, "I will keep him!"
Indirect: The princess said that she would keep him.
Direct: Miriam asked, "Shall I find you a nurse?"
Indirect: Miriam asked if she should find a nurse.
📌 Adjectives for describing feelings
Good writers use strong adjectives. Notice these from the story:
A dreadful command / a terrible idea / a fine, strong baby / a warm, soft blanket / a gentle rocking
Try: Describe the basket using 3 adjectives.

Ideas & Society
Big questions — think freely
💝
A mother put her baby in a river to save him. What does this story tell us about the love between parents and children? Can you think of any modern examples of parents sacrificing for their children?
⚖️
The pharaoh's daughter disobeyed her father's law. Are there situations where breaking a law is the right thing to do? What makes a law unjust?
🧠
Miriam was very clever and quick-thinking. Why is quick thinking important in dangerous situations? Can you think of a time when thinking fast helped someone?
🌊
Rivers appear many times in this story. In what ways are rivers important to human life? Think about: water, food, transport, and civilization.
